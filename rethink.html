Designing a Modern Day Reading Experience

rethink ipad magazine

Recent years have brought a plethora of devices into our lives. How we interact with and read information on each of them is significantly different. So how can a magazine best adapt to the tablet reading experience?

Research

To begin the project, I wanted to explore what made reading digitally a fundamentally different experience from reading a traditional print magazine. I did not want to just directly translate a magazine's content into a PDF on an iPad.

What are the Key Factors that differentiate
an interactive reading experience?

Many of the traditional magazines have been struggling to translate their publications into digital formats, so I wanted to briefly explore the concepts that make an interactive reading experience fundamentally different from the print one.

Competitive Analysis

In addition to the digital context that the magazine would exist in, I also wanted to take a look at the variety of editioral styles that exist now. It is exciting to see how institutions that have been around for 100 years adapt to compete with the numerous sources of distractions for modern readers.
The succesful traditional organizations have found a way to maintain their established voice and authority while allowing their information to spread through the internet.
There are many interesting new digital publishing sites, an example of which is Medium, which describes itself as the place for "everyone's thoughts and ideas." It is a place where you can see an article written by Barack Obama living alongside someone's rambling description of Peruvian foods.

Designing Editorial Content for today is an exciting challenge, a balance between thoughtful, curated content, and an experience engaging enough to keep readers from slipping away to watch cat videos.

Branding

With a mind full of the exciting things being done with interactive reading, I chose a traditional print magazine, Discover, as a case study to explore modern reading.

Discover magazine aims to be a place where readers can explore what the future could look like. The visual style and digital presence of the magazine don't live up to this goal, with cluttered layouts and an editorial voice that seems designed to be more at home in a dentist's office than a treasured place on someone's shelf (or iPad).

How could Discover magazine better represent the innovative
ideas it strives to present?

The Name & The Visuals

Rethink is changes the focus of the magazine to one that questions what is, and looks to the future to imagine how we can reimagine the world we live in. It takes an active stance on issues, and doesn't just present them as they are. The type is designed for boldness and modernity, while still looking approachable and optimistic.

Designing the Magazine

Information Architecture

Rethink is structured around a collection of topics such as the city, crime or intelligence. To create a unified, engaging reading experience, these broad topics are narrowed with a thought provoking question, like "How can our cities be redefined?" The content within is an exploration into the exciting future of an important topic.

Sketching Interfaces

Exploring how to seamlessly transition from a topic to the different types of content within, emphasizing the idea of rethinking a specific issue
