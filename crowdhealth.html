Bringing Community into Healthcare

Crowdhealth Product Concept

The American healthcare system spends massive amounts of money on healthcare, yet has mediocre results. Part of the reason our healthcare is unsatisfactory is the lack of attention we pay to the aspects of health that happen outside of the clinic, like housing, access to food and social support. Crowdhealth is an early concept to begin to address these issues for the California Healthcare Foundation's Contest, Putting Care in Context.

Intital Concept Presentation

The goal of crowdhealth is to make connecting patients to community resources as easy as prescribing a pill.

Link to Contest Description and Problem Statement
Building on the 100+ Interviews and Clinic Observations at the Mayo Clinic, as well as the exposure to building electronic medical records at Kareo, I came up with an early idea to better integrate social resources into a clinic visit. The Presentation below explains the basic ideas behind Crowdhealth.

Early Prototyping

The first presentation for Crowdhealth was made in a very short time frame, and there were many more concepts I wanted to explore. Further building out the workflow and tools that would allow a provider to refer social resources during a normal clinic visit was an exciting concept to me, so I created some early workflows and screens for the application.

My presentation for the Putting Care in Context Contest was full of compelling ideas, but I wanted to get an idea of what crowdhealth with look like in reality.

Reflections

Crowdhealth was a great way to summarize everything I learned from Kareo and the Mayo Clinic, and is a statement about what healthcare can become when it embraces both community and technology.
